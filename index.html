About Me

I am a passionate computational biologist dedicated to advancing science through innovative data analysis. My journey began with a Ph.D. in Neuroscience at the University of Nevada, Reno, where I developed a passion for understanding through data, I am currently a bioinformatics scientist at the Nevada Bioinformatics Center where I work with researchers across that state in data analysis and trainings.

Since August 2016, my husband and I have been living off-grid in a tiny house, reflecting our commitment to sustainable living. We share our experiences on our website, Specialized Tinys, where I write about tiny house living and off-grid solutions, offering practical tips and advice.

This unique blend of high-tech bioinformatics and sustainable living has honed my problem-solving skills and resourcefulness. Whether designing bioinformatic analysis or creating sustainable living solutions, I am driven by a passion for impactful innovation and lifelong learning. Join me on my journey as I explore the intersections of science, sustainability, and minimalist living.
